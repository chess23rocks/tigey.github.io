GEOGRAPHY:

Virginia's geography is full of fertile soil. Virginia's landscape also includes plentiful forests, hilly areas, and even swampy marshes.

RELIGION:

Virginia did not have a dominant religion. Therefore, Virginia had religious freedom, and some of the religions practiced in Virginia included Baptism, Anglicanism, and more.

TRADE AND NATURAL RESOURCES:

Virginia's exports/trading goods including many forms of agriculture. The hot and humid weather made it possible for farmers to plant crops all year long. Also, the fertile soil helped the farmers too. Some of the cash crops they planted included tobacco, cotton, corn, grain, and many different varieties of fruits and veggies. Some other natural resources found in Virginia were fish and timber/lumber.

MAJOR CITIES:

Virginia had four major cities Williamsburg, Jamestown, Richmond, and Petersburg.

HISTORY:

1607- Jamestown was founded by the Virginia Comapny and John Smith. They founded Virginia to try to make some profits from trade and farming. They soon found out tobacco, a major cash crop in England, grew very well in Virginia due to the hot and humid weather and the nutrient-rich soil.
