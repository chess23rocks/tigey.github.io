RELIGION

No religious persecution was found in New York because there was complete religious freedom in New York.

TRADE

New York, A.K.A "the breadbasket," produced large amounts of wheat, which was grounded into flour. Also, New York produced pork, beef, and more livestock. New York also exported timber, iron ore, furs, and even coal.

History

New York was orriginally a Dutch colony named New Amsterdam, but later, the British took over in 1664. The British soon re-named the colony, the name we still know of, New York. New York was named after the York of England.
